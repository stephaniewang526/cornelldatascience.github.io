Formula One Performance Prediction


"Which is the best team in F1 racing?"
"What factors largely influence a team’s performance in a given Grand Prix?"
"Does the driver’s ability or experience affect the team performance?"
"Are some teams favourites in certain races?"
"How significant is the car's performance in racing?"
We are interested in answering these questions through a combination of Data Mining & Machine Learning methods.

Formula One (AKA F1), and currently officially referred to as the FIA Formula One World Championship, is the highest class of auto racing sanctioned by the Federation Internationale de l’Automobile (FIA).
The "formula" in the name refers to a set of rules to which all participants’ cars must comply.
The F1 season consists of a series of races, known as Grand Prix, held on purpose-built circuits, and to a lesser extent, former public roads and closed city streets. The results of each race are combined to determine two annual World Championships, one for the drivers and one for the manufacturers.

We are currently parsing data available on the Formula One official website. We intend to adjust the data, taking into account the stability in the number of Grand Prix races over time, new team additions, changes to points systems, as well as other factors which may cause the differences in the data. We plan on comparing the performance of KNN, Decision Trees, & Support Vector Machine models in regards to learning and predicting the team performances. We also plan to incorporate elements of a Hidden Markov Model to account for changes in a driver's performance over time.
